Why Ethereum Needed Arbitrum

Ethereum's success created its biggest problem: congestion. Layer 2 solutions like Arbitrum solve this elegantly.

🚧

The Problem

Ethereum mainnet processes ~15 TPS. During peak demand, gas fees can exceed $100 per transaction — pricing out everyday users completely.

// ethereum bottleneck
⚡

What is Arbitrum

Arbitrum is an Optimistic Rollup that batches thousands of transactions off-chain, then posts compressed proofs to Ethereum — inheriting its security.

// offchain.labs
💸

Real Benefit

Fees drop from $50–$100 to under $0.10. Swaps on Uniswap that cost $60 on mainnet cost cents on Arbitrum — same security, 100x cheaper.

Web3 Concepts Decoded

Side-by-side comparisons of the core ideas powering the decentralized revolution.

🌐

Web2 vs Web3

The evolution of the internet
⚠ Web2 — The Old Internet
Ownership Corporations own your data. Google, Meta, and Amazon hold massive vaults of personal information with little user control.
Trust Model Trust depends on centralized servers. If the company shuts down or gets hacked, your data and access vanish.
Monetization Your attention and data are the product. Platforms profit by selling targeted advertising based on your behavior.
Example Twitter, YouTube, Facebook — you build an audience but the platform can ban you instantly, taking your reach with it.
VS
✓ Web3 — The New Internet
Ownership You control your own data via cryptographic keys. Your wallet is your identity — no platform can revoke access.
Trust Model Code is law. Smart contracts execute automatically — no human intermediary, no possibility of corruption or downtime.
Monetization Creators earn directly from their community via tokens, NFTs, and DeFi protocols with zero platform commission.
Example Lens Protocol, Farcaster — censorship-resistant social with verifiable ownership of followers and content on-chain.
₿

Ethereum vs Bitcoin

The two titans of crypto
₿ Bitcoin
PurposeDigital gold — a peer-to-peer electronic cash system and store of value. Built for one thing: trustless value transfer.
SupplyHard-capped at 21 million BTC forever. Scarcity by design — no central bank can inflate it away.
ConsensusProof of Work (PoW). Miners burn energy to validate blocks — battle-tested since 2009.
Smart ContractsVery limited. Script-based, intentionally minimal. Security through simplicity.
VS
Ξ Ethereum
PurposeA world computer — a programmable blockchain for decentralized apps. ETH is the fuel for this global computation engine.
SupplyUncapped but deflationary since EIP-1559. Burned transaction fees can make ETH net negative supply.
ConsensusProof of Stake (PoS) since The Merge (2022). 99.95% less energy — validators stake ETH as collateral.
Smart ContractsTuring-complete Solidity contracts power DeFi, NFTs, DAOs, and the entire Web3 ecosystem.
🔑

Public Key vs Private Key

The foundation of cryptographic identity
Property 🔓 Public Key 🔒 Private Key
What It Is Your wallet address — shareable identifier derived mathematically from your private key A 256-bit secret number. The master key to your entire crypto identity. Never share this.
Analogy Your bank account number — give it out to receive money Your PIN + the physical card combined — the one thing that proves ownership
Share with others? ✓ Yes, always safe ✗ Never, ever
Generated From Derived from the private key using elliptic curve multiplication (one-way) Randomly generated — 256 bits of pure entropy. Yours to control.
Use Case Receiving funds, verifying signatures, identifying your wallet on-chain Signing transactions, proving ownership, accessing funds
If Lost/Stolen No issue — it's public anyway. Anyone can see it on Etherscan. 🚨 Catastrophic. All funds permanently inaccessible or stolen.
🗄️

Blockchain vs Traditional Databases

Why not just use MySQL?
🗄 Traditional Database
StructureCentralized server managed by one entity (AWS, Google Cloud). One admin can alter, delete, or corrupt all records.
PerformanceBlazing fast — can handle millions of reads/writes per second. ACID-compliant transactions.
TrustYou trust the company not to tamper with records. There's no way to independently verify data integrity.
Best ForApplications requiring speed, complex queries, and where you trust the operator — banking apps, e-commerce, SaaS.
VS
⛓ Blockchain
StructureDistributed across thousands of nodes. No single entity controls it. Records are cryptographically linked and immutable.
PerformanceSlower — consensus mechanisms take time. Ethereum does ~15-30 TPS vs millions for traditional DBs.
TrustTrustless by design. Anyone can verify every transaction in history. Math guarantees integrity, not promises.
Best ForFinancial settlements, supply chain tracking, voting systems — anywhere transparency and censorship resistance matter.
